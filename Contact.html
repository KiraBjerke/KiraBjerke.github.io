Frequently Asked Questions

Board Of Directors

What are the offices of the Board of Directors?

In general, the HOA Board of Directors will have the offices of President, Vice President, Secretary, Treasurer, and Member at Large. The officers are usually elected at the first Board meeting by the Board following the annual association meeting. The term of office for a Board member typically lasts one year. The duties for each office are:

President

  • Presides at all HOA Board meetings
  • Makes sure orders and resolutions are carried out
  • Signs contracts

Vice-President

  • Takes the place of the President when he/she is absent.
  • Performs other duties as required.

Secretary

  • Keeps meeting minutes
  • Records votes

Treasurer

  • Receives and deposits monies
  • Signs checks
  • Prepares the annual budget

Member at Large

  • Participate in Board voting and discussions
  • Performs duties as assigned
  • Serve on Architectural Control Committee

What Other Duties Does an HOA Board Have?

  • Arrange for the care and upkeep of the commons areas;
  • Set the amount of and collect HOA fees each year;
  • Prepare a summary of the accomplishments for the year, including a summary of fiscal expenditures;
  • Prepare a budget for the next fiscal year for submission to the membership;
  • Enforce any and all covenants, restrictions, agreements, and rules and regulations to protect the homeowners, relating to the use of common areas and personal conduct of HOA members
  • Regulate, plan, arrange for and approve all landscaping, improvements, erosion control, aesthetic and architectural issues, usage of the commons areas, and all questions concerning maintenance, repair, improvements, changes, or additions to the commons areas;
  • Secure insurance as may be necessary or advisable

When does the Board meet?

The BOD generally meets at 8:00 PM on the second Monday of each month in the library at Carrie Busey School. See the calendar for current scheduling. The Board may also meet at other times as deemed necessary by the President, and conducts business via email, too. All email items are discussed at the regular monthly board meeting, so that the meeting minutes reflect emailed action. Agendas, minutes, and other related documents from the BOD meetings are available on the Meetings page.

About the Covenants

Covenants are legal documents that lay out the guidelines for the planned community.

The Covenants are recorded in the county records in the county where the property is located and are legally binding. This means that when you purchase a lot or a home in Prairie Fields you automatically become a member of the HOA. The Covenants, which were established by the builder, are the rules of the Prairie Fields neighborhood. They govern what you can, cannot, or must do with respect to your home.

Which covenants apply to my phase?

Each covenant applies to a single Phase. See the Prairie Field Phase page to find out what Phase your house belongs to. To access the Covenant files, click here.

How are covenants enforced?

At the 9/20/10 Board of Directors meeting, the Board officially approved a Covenant Enforcement Policy. The purpose of the policy is to clarify the way in which the Board plans to enforce covenants, and to clarify the procedures for enforcement of the covenants. At this time, only complaints which are brought to the Board and which comply with the policy will be acted on.

Form to request covenant enforcement

Covenants will be enforced by the Board if there is a specific complaint submitted to the board according to the Covenant Enforcement Policy. If you would like to make a complaint or covenant enforcement request to the HOA Board, please fill out the Covenant Enforcement Form. Note that the information submitted will not be published on this website, but it will be used by the Board of Directors as they investigate the complaint.
